Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI or online payment fraud is a type of cybercrime where criminals trick people into sending money through apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. It is becoming very common because many people use digital payments daily.

This fraud usually happens step-by-step in a simple but clever way. First, the scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, message, or social media. They may pretend to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer or seller. Next, they create a sense of urgency or trust. Then, they send a payment request or a fake link and ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN, saying it is needed to receive money. In reality, entering the PIN approves sending money, not receiving it. Once the victim enters the PIN, the money gets transferred to the scammer’s account.

Students, elderly people, and those who are not very familiar with technology are usually the main targets. People who are in a hurry or not careful also fall into this trap easily.

The consequences can be serious. Victims may lose a large amount of money, and it can be very difficult to recover it. It also causes stress and loss of trust in online systems.

This case shows the importance of being careful while using online payment apps and never sharing your UPI PIN with anyone.
